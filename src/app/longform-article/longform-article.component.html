Longform Article

Creating a responsive web design for the longform article:
"When Awareness is Not Enough"

CHALLENGE

The article focuses on how simply being aware of the problem of homelessness is not enough to manage the issue. It also discusses how homelessness is situational, and the factors leading up to housing insecurity differ between individuals. It was important to clearly convey that homelessness is an issue that impacts individuals in unique ways.


IDEATION & RESEARCH


COLOR PALETTE

The article’s focus on the individual stories of people experiencing homelessness illustrated that homelessness is a very complex issue. This led me to conceptualize a grayscale design with a single color, to show that while the causes and solutions to issue may seem obvious (black and white) on the surface, there are many different underlying factors (shades of gray). The article seeks to highlight the individuals behind homelessness, illustrated through the use of color.


TYPE STUDIES

When choosing typefaces, I needed typography that was web-friendly and also evoked the seriousness of the article. For the headings, I wanted a modern take on a classic typeface to show that the homelessness is persistent, serious, and still relevant. I looked at both serif and and sans-serif options, and created type studies to help me compare typefaces. And choose the most relevant and suitable option.



LAYOUT

When constructing the overall layout of the article, I had to consider a web page structure that was engaging, highlighted the key points of the article, and encouraged sharing of the information with other potential users.

I began by analyzing existing news and information sharing websites, and then moved on to craft five different wireframes.

Final Result

When developing the final composition, I also had to make sure it was responsive on mobile browsers. So, I went through the website and visualized how the sections of the website would resize and stack relative to one another to fit on the screen while maintaining the same reading experience.
