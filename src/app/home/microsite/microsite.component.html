Music Festival Website

A responsive website advertising the imagined music
festival, Archipelago. Hosted in Hawaii, USA.

CHALLENGE

With a fairly open design brief, we were tasked with imagining an event inspired by a podcast. I decided to create a website for a music festival in Hawaii, named Archipelago. As a website for an elaboarte music festival hosted over a week across the Hawaiian Islands, I was challenged with creating a website that mimicked the contemporary image of the artists performing while providing details about the many events and locations incorporated in the festival.


IDEATION & RESEARCH


INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

I began by drafting a mind map of the site’s components and attributes. Once I had an idea of the elements that were going to be added to the website, I reorganized the site into the following IA, helping sort the content and distinguish the layout and navigation of the site.


TYPE STUDIES

The typography for this website demanded a modern, sans-serif typeface to reflect the popular artists performing at the festival. I spent time experimenting with playful typefaces that reflected both the modern music industry and the tropical Hawaiian setting.



WIREFRAMES

The next step was to draft wireframes for the major internal website pages. I chose to focus specifically on the landing, featured artists, and event calendar pages.



DESIGN

Once the wireframes were completed, I moved on to designing the web pages. Creating individual components in XD was important in creating a cohesive design for the final website.



PROTOTYPE

I created interactive prototypes in Adobe XD of both the mobile and desktop variatons of the app.
