Bodoni Type
Specimen Booklet

A booklet designed to showcase Bodoni, one of the first modern typefaces.

CHALLENGE

Creating a booklet to showcase the easily recognizable, typeface Bodoni, required research into its history, and the impact it had as one of the first modern typefaces. Understanding its relevance to popular culture and the design world was a necessary foundation to creating a booklet where Bodoni was the star. With few images other than the letterform, this booklet was designed to highlight the unique qualities and beautiful typographic design that went into creating the many variations of this fundamental typeface.


IDEATION & RESEARCH

TYPE STUDIES

I spent most of the research time investigating type studies and viewing different arrangements of the typeface.

LAYOUTS

I created two different compositions of the booklet, each with four different spreads. After gathering critique and feedback on both documents, I revised the layouts and selected the six best spreads to be included in the final design.

Layout 1

I chose the first variation for several reasons. The vector image of Giambattista Bodoni did not match the design of the rest of the booklet, and for this reason I chose not to include it. His name on the page next to the vector image is also too large. I ultimately decided to remove the name from the left page, as it was bolded in the paragraph on the second page.

Layout 2

While comparing these two spreads, I decided to move forward with the first variation because I thought the diagonal type was a more interesting use of space. Furthermore, the foundaries on the right side of the second layout felt like an afterthought, and I wanted to feature them elsewhere.

Layout 3

Moving forward with these pages, I wanted to make sure the shoe with the alphabet would be bigger and featured in the final composition. I also wanted to add more brands to the showcase to show how prominent Bodoni is in fashion and pop culture.

Layout 4

For the final spreads in each of my compositions, I chose to feature the full-page of numbers, rather than hiding them in the triangle. The numbers within the triangle also felt haphazardly thrown together, while the large, in-line layout created a sense of order and let the reader appreciate the letterform. I also decided to remove the type in the middle as it was distracting, and move the numbers closer together.
