MAC Cosmetics Website Redesign

A responsive website advertising the imagined music
festival, Archipelago. Hosted in Hawaii, USA.

CHALLENGE

The major challenge for this redesign project was to take the website of a well-established, existing brand, and make it more user-friendly and modern. I began by exploring the existing website and social media in order to best determine how to use the assets already available to create a site that stayed true to the MAC Cosmetics brand and effectively promoted their products.

PROJECT FOCUS

After assessing the website, I chose to focus on the homepage and a template for internal product pages. As this was a redesign proposal, I thought these two pages were the most vital in displaying the look and feel of the direction the site could move in.



EXISTING HOMEPAGE REVIEW

I had the following impressions of the existing homepage:

Layout

The layout did seem to stick to a four-column grid, which was evident in the layout of each div on the site. However, the proportions of items within each column were off, and the grid was broken quite frequently. I wanted to redesign a layout that stuck with the baseline grid in order to make each section and page seem like cohesive elements to the site, rather than standalone designs.

Color Scheme

Red and green are difficult choices for the screen, as they are vibrant colors that are opposites on the color wheel. Although the “Hypnotizing Holiday” promotion was being run, the graphic elements overwhelmed the products and made the website unappealing to people trying to purchase makeup products.

MAC is also sold at department stores and seen as a generally more sophisticated and expensive brand. The colors used here take away from the brand’s overall appeal, and I wanted to bring the focus back to the high quality holdiay products being offered.

Banner Ads

The banner ads used in this website seemed to be piled at the top of the page, and were not separated from the main body of the site. It felt as though they were being created separately and added to the website rather than considering how they contribute to the overall design of the page. Furthermore, the products are hidden behind layers of extra graphics, taking away from the effectiveness of the advertisements.

Menu Bar

I thought that the menu bar was well-designed overall. However, I did not love that there were additional banner ads running across the top of the menu, which was distracting. Anchoring the menu to the top of the screen was my main goal when considering this component.


EXISTING SHOPPING PAGE REVIEW

I had the following impressions of the internal shopping page:

Layout

I did not like the hand-writtten text that breaks the grid and describes the different eye shadows. The paint pot banner also seems out of place, especially since it is next to the full-screen banner header. The actual product descriptions are separated from the banner ad for the product and seems disconnected.

Typography

The type on the header is very hawrd to read. The white text with reduced opacity makes it very hard to see the different sections on the page. Furthermore, there is a lack of consistency between the type weights and colors in the links, headings. and buttons.

Advertisements

I wanted to focus the advertisements on the products, make the type in each section more legible, and make the product name more prominent.
